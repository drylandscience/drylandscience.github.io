About

I am a PhD student that is working on dryland ecogeomorphology with a penchant for quantitative analysis, spectroscopy, and problem solving. My research primarily focuses on aeolian processes ecological assessments atmospheric processes, ecohydrology, geography, and dryland ecology. This array of research has provided me with experience working with dust storms,

vegetative and ecohydrological models, experimental vegetative and climate datasets, and aeolian models of soil moisture and dust emission. The goal of my research aims to improve decision making in land management and preparedness in response to changing vegetative patterns based on our understanding of climate change.
